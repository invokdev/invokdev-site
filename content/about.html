What is Linux?

So what is Linux? Linux can be a confusing topic, and it can take hours or even days to get a correct answer as to what Linux is. In short, Linux is an operating system, but mainly it's a kernel that's used to tell the computer's hardware what to do and how to do it. The linux kernel is the most important part, as it runs the entire operating system, and makes your computer function correctly. The common comparission that's used is that the linux kernel is like the engine of a car, without it the car won't function correctly.


What's a Distrobution?

A distrobution is the other piece of the operating system, that you'll interact with on a daily basis. In essence the distobution is more or less the operating system you'll be using. Each linux distrobution is different than the next, however they're all fairly similar. There are main distrobutions and then various forks of each distrobution, giving you a limitless amount of options to choose from. The distrobution will include most of the software you will use, as well as the desktop environment that you'll interact with. If we revert back to the car analogy, you can think of the distrobution as being the rest of the car that you're driving. Yes all the finishing pieces are important, however it'd still get nowhere with out the engine to drive it.
